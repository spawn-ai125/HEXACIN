📟 Product Introduction: What is HEXACIN? HEXACIN is a zero-training deterministic expert system architecture developed from scratch for critical medical diagnostics and differential diagnosis processes. Unlike today's multi-billion parameter AI models (LLMs), it has no dependency on weight matrices, neural networks, or the cloud. It uses a two-stage inference engine that filters medical data with pure computer science logic. The risk of hallucinations (information fabrication), which is unacceptable in the medical world, is mathematically $0% in the HEXACIN architecture. 🧬 What are the Differences from its Ancestor MYCIN (1976)? HEXACIN is the direct modern sibling and technological successor of the MYCIN expert system, which changed the history of medicine and artificial intelligence at Stanford University in the 1970s. Both systems share the philosophy that "AI should reason, not predict." However, HEXACIN, backed by 50 years of accumulated computer science knowledge, offers the following fundamental differences: 1. MYCIN's Ability to Diagnose Unknown/Mutated Diseases: If the pathogen or virus in the patient is outside the ~500 rules in MYCIN's database, the system crashes, gives an error, or makes a misdiagnosis. HEXACIN: When known templates fail, it activates the "Homeostatic Drift Analysis" layer. It creates a map of deviations from the vital limits of the human body (pH, fever, blood pressure, etc.). It instantly detects even mutations that the scientific world has not yet named and synthesizes a clinical protocol from scratch with an emergency ICD-10 U99.9 code. 2. Double-Layered Injection Instead of Backward Chaining: MYCIN: It had a cumbersome backward-chaining architecture that asked the user hundreds of questions one after another, such as "Does the patient have a fever?", "What is the blood count?". HEXACIN: It swallows a single JSON or Dictionary data packet (biomarkers and vital signs) in microseconds. It instantly generates a decision by simultaneously processing the data through two different logic filters. 3. Precise Biological Limits Instead of Certainty Factors: MYCIN: Used "certainty factors" between $0.0 and $1.0 based on human estimations when making diagnoses. As these probabilities were multiplied in deep logic trees, they could lead to large systemic errors. HEXACIN: Directly connects fuzzy logic principles to the strict lower/upper limits of human anatomy and homeostasis. It produces mathematically verifiable and precise results. 4. Agent and IDE Integration Compatibility: MYCIN: Was a monolithic structure that only ran on the command lines of older mainframes. HEXACIN: Produces only structured pure data protocols (JSON) as output. This allows it to be directly integrated as an API into AI native IDEs like Cursor, hospital automation systems, or wearable medical telemetry devices.
